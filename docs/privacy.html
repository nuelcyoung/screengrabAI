Privacy Policy

ScreenGrab AI Chrome Extension

Last Updated: February 21, 2026

1. Introduction

Hi there. I built ScreenGrab AI with privacy as a core principle, not an afterthought. This policy explains what happens with your data when you use the extension.

Quick summary: By default, everything stays on your machine. If you choose to use cloud AI providers, only your screenshot goes to them—not to me.

2. What Data Gets Handled

Screenshots and Captured Content

When you capture a screenshot, the image data lives temporarily in your browser's memory. It's gone once the analysis finishes. I don't store it anywhere.

API Keys and Settings

If you configure cloud AI providers like OpenAI or Anthropic, your API keys get saved locally on your device using Chrome's built-in storage. They never touch any server I control.

User Preferences

Your extension settings stay on your device. That's it.

3. How Your Data Gets Used

Your screenshots are used strictly for:

  • Pulling text out of images (OCR)
  • Providing AI analysis and insights about what you captured
  • Answering follow-up questions you ask about the content

I don't use anything for advertising, marketing, or anything beyond making the extension work for you.

4. Third-Party Services

Local Processing (Recommended)

If you set up Ollama or another local AI model, everything happens on your computer. Nothing leaves your device. This is the most private option.

Cloud Processing (Optional)

When you choose to use cloud AI providers, your screenshot images get sent directly to them:

  • OpenAI — For GPT-4 Vision and text analysis (Privacy Policy)
  • Anthropic — For Claude text analysis (Privacy Policy)
  • Google Gemini — For vision and text analysis (Privacy Policy)
  • Google Cloud Vision — For OCR (Privacy Policy)

Each provider handles data according to their own policies. I'd recommend reading them if you're concerned about how they process things.

5. Data Storage and Retention

  • Screenshots: Processed in memory, then gone. Not retained.
  • API Keys: Stored locally until you remove them or uninstall the extension.
  • Settings: Stored locally until you clear them or uninstall.

I don't run any servers or databases that hold your personal data. There's nothing to breach.

6. Data Sharing

I don't sell, rent, or share your data with anyone. Your information only goes somewhere when:

  • You explicitly configure a cloud AI provider
  • I'm legally required to (which hasn't happened and hopefully never will)

7. Your Rights

You're in complete control:

  • Access: All your data is local. Check Chrome's developer tools if you want to see it.
  • Deletion: Uninstall the extension and everything goes away.
  • Portability: Export through Chrome's extension management.
  • Opt-out: Use Ollama (local) and nothing ever leaves your device.

8. Security

Here's what I do to keep things secure:

  • API keys are stored using Chrome's secure storage API
  • No data moves anywhere without you setting it up first
  • Local processing keeps sensitive documents... local

9. Children's Privacy

ScreenGrab AI isn't meant for kids under 13. I don't knowingly collect anything from children.

10. International Users

If you're outside the US, cloud providers might process your data in different countries. Using local processing (Ollama) keeps everything within your device, regardless of where you live.

11. Changes to This Policy

I might update this from time to time. If anything significant changes, I'll update the date at the top.
